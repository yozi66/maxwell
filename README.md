Maxwell's equations
===================

```math
\begin{align}
\vec{\nabla} \cdot \vec{D} &= P \\
\vec{\nabla} \cdot B &= 0 \\
\vec{\nabla} \times \vec{E} &= - \frac{\partial}{\partial t} \vec B \\
\vec{\nabla} \times \vec{H} &= \frac{\partial}{\partial t} \vec D + \vec{j}
\end{align}
```

These are **Maxwell’s equations written in macroscopic (matter-inclusive) form**, using the fields **E, D, B, H**. Let's see **what each equation means physically**, and the description of symbols.

---

## 1. Gauss’s law for electricity

```math
\vec{\nabla} \cdot \vec{D} = P
```

**Meaning:**
The divergence of the electric displacement field **D** equals the **free charge density**.

* $\vec{D}$: electric displacement field
* $P$: free charge density (often written $\rho_f$)

**Physical interpretation:**
Electric charges are **sources or sinks of electric flux**. In materials, bound charges are absorbed into $\vec{D}$, so only **free charges** appear explicitly.

---

## 2. Gauss’s law for magnetism

```math
\vec{\nabla} \cdot \vec{B} = 0
```

**Meaning:**
There are **no magnetic monopoles**.

* $\vec{B}$: magnetic flux density

**Physical interpretation:**
Magnetic field lines **always form closed loops**. You never get an isolated “north” or “south” magnetic charge.

---

## 3. Faraday’s law of induction

```math
\vec{\nabla} \times \vec{E} = - \frac{\partial \vec{B}}{\partial t}
```

**Meaning:**
A **time-varying magnetic field** produces a **circulating electric field**.

**Physical interpretation:**
This is the principle behind:

* electric generators
* transformers
* electromagnetic induction

The minus sign reflects **Lenz’s law**: the induced electric field opposes the change that created it.

---

## 4. Ampère–Maxwell law

```math
\vec{\nabla} \times \vec{H} = \frac{\partial \vec{D}}{\partial t} + \vec{j}
```

**Meaning:**
Magnetic fields are generated by:

1. **Electric currents** ($\vec{j}$)
2. **Time-varying electric fields** ($\partial \vec{D}/\partial t$)

* $\vec{H}$: magnetic field intensity
* $\vec{j}$: free current density

**Physical interpretation:**
Maxwell’s correction ($\partial \vec{D}/\partial t$) allows electromagnetic waves to exist and ensures charge conservation.

---

## Field meanings summary

| Symbol    | Name                     | Role                       |
| --------- | ------------------------ | -------------------------- |
| $\vec{E}$ | Electric field           | Force on charges           |
| $\vec{D}$ | Electric displacement    | Accounts for polarization  |
| $\vec{B}$ | Magnetic flux density    | Acts on moving charges     |
| $\vec{H}$ | Magnetic field intensity | Accounts for magnetization |
| $P$       | Free charge density      | Source of $\vec{D}$        |
| $\vec{j}$ | Free current density     | Source of $\vec{H}$        |

---

## Why use this form?

This formulation is especially useful:

* in **materials** (dielectrics, magnetics)
* in **engineering electromagnetics**
* when separating **free** and **bound** charges/currents
